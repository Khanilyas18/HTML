• Introduction and Definition World Heritage Sites:

  • Preserving Cultural and Natural Treasures The world is home to countless cultural and natural treasures that are of immense value to humanity. These treasures, known as World Heritage Sites, are recognized by the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) for their outstanding universal value. From ancient cities to breathtaking natural wonders, these sites are a testament to the diversity and richness of our planet's cultural and natural heritage. World Heritage Sites are cultural or natural sites that are considered to be of outstanding universal value. These sites can be monuments, cities, landscapes, or natural features that are recognized for their cultural, historical, scientific, or natural significance. To be designated as a World Heritage Site, a location must meet certain criteria, such as representing a masterpiece of human creative genius, exhibiting an important interchange of human values over time, or containing unique or exceptional natural features. There are three types of World Heritage Sites: cultural, natural, and mixed. Cultural Heritage Sites are recognized for their cultural significance, such as historic cities, monuments, and archaeological sites. Natural Heritage Sites are recognized for their natural beauty and significance, such as national parks, wildlife reserves, and unique natural features.
